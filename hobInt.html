Hobbies and Interests

Everyone has things they like to do or would like to do in their free time. These can range from just playing games to horse riding. In my opinion, having a hobby is pertinent to one's mental health and psychological stability, as it can be considered a net to fall back on whenever you feel too stressed. A way of releasing that stress is very important.

As I cannot think of anything else to say here, let us count down the top 5 interests of mine.

  1. Coding
    2. Starting off the list, we have coding. Mainly coding for fun, or just easy questions in general. Why would one enjoy it, you might ask. To answer that question, I shall redirect you to the High School chapter.

    As an example, recently we have been assigned an assignment in Fundamentals. Though unnecessary, I wrote a 1967-line code to submit that you can view here.

    Was there a point in coding such a ridiculously long project? No. Do I get more points for it? Hopefully. But that is the point of a hobby: It is for fun.

    Finally, though it is fun, I cannot just code the entire day without feeling tired or worn out, so coding is superseded by the things below.

  2. Movies
    3. I have a special rule for movies. If it is in English or Vietnamese, I let it run like normal, probably because I understand what they are saying.

    If it is in any other language though, I will fast forward it to 2x speed, saving a lot of time. Quite a couple of my friends disapprove of this method, but I enjoy it immensely.

    I would not say I have a favorite, since after I watched something I rarely come back to it, but in general, I prefer Japanese animation over all others. Maybe it is because they can portray things as dark as they are without having to worry about censorship.

    The longest consecutive period of time that I have spent watching movies are probably about 5 hours, which pales in comparison to the entries below.

  3. Reading
    4. I started reading at a very young age, both English and Vietnamese, so I can read quite quickly. This allows me to tear through books much quicker than normal people would.

    My favorite genre would be psychological thriller or psychological crime-solving, if that is what it is called. Their complicated yet exceptionally crafted stories have engraved themselves on my brain.

    Once I pick up a book that interests me, I only stop for food or when I am forced to sleep. I believe the longest time I spent reading was 7 hours continuously, delaying lunch back then. Some of the longer books that I have read stretches into days of reading, though not continuously of course.

  4. Music
    5. Music can be considered an integral part of my life, used when I code, read books or play games. The only days I do not listen to music would be the days that I was sick.

    My taste in music is unique, with my main genre being Deathstep. A mixture of Dubstep and Death metal, it is most likely the most brutal "music" you can find anywhere, although I do listen to other genres as well.

    That is about it about music: An integral part of my life, not much to say.

  5. Games
    6. Perhaps it is a surprise, perhaps not. I play a couple of games, but I do dedicate towards them.

    Whenever I find a game that I really like, I spend a lot of time researching the game: Character, lore, meta, ... Aside from the games I play, and I watch a lot more.

    I would not say I have a favorite genre in games, very inconsistent across the board. I can control my playing so that it does not affect my day-to-day life at all, but that was not the case when I was 13. If you are interested, you can read the High School chapter.
These five hobbies shaped my life as I know it, and have transformed me into what I am today. Love them or hate them, I am quite thankful for them.
